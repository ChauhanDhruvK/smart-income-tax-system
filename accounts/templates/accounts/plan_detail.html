Overview

ELSS (Equity Linked Savings Scheme) is a mutual fund that invests primarily in equities. It offers the dual benefit of high return potential and a tax deduction under Section 80C. With the shortest lock-in of just 3 years among all 80C instruments, it suits investors with a moderate-to-high risk appetite looking to grow wealth while saving tax.

Key Features
Tax Deduction up to ₹1,50,000
Invest any amount up to ₹1.5L and claim the full deduction under Section 80C.
Shortest Lock-in: 3 Years
Among all 80C instruments, ELSS has the shortest mandatory holding period.
Market-linked Returns
Historically delivers 12–15% annualised returns. Not guaranteed but outperforms most fixed instruments over the long term.
SIP Option Available
Invest monthly through a Systematic Investment Plan starting from just ₹500/month.
How to Get Started
1
Complete KYC

2
Choose a Fund
Compare top-rated ELSS funds by 3-year and 5-year returns, expense ratio, and fund manager track record.
3
Invest Lump Sum or via SIP
Invest directly on the AMC website or through Groww, Zerodha, or your bank's investment portal.
4
Save the Proof
Download your investment statement and submit it to your employer during the declaration window.

Overview

PPF is a long-term savings instrument backed by the Government of India. It falls under the EEE (Exempt-Exempt-Exempt) category — contributions are deductible, interest is tax-free, and the maturity amount is also tax-free. It is the safest 80C option, ideal for conservative investors focused on wealth preservation with guaranteed, risk-free returns.

Key Features
Triple Tax Exemption (EEE)
Deposit, interest earned, and the final maturity amount are all completely tax-free.
Zero Risk — Sovereign Guarantee
Backed by the Government of India. Absolute capital protection with no default risk.
Loan & Partial Withdrawal
Loans available from the 3rd year. Partial withdrawals are permitted after the 7th year.
Extendable in 5-Year Blocks
After the 15-year maturity, extend in blocks of 5 years with or without fresh contributions.
How to Get Started
1
Visit a Bank or Post Office
Open at SBI, HDFC, ICICI, PNB, or any post office. Most banks also offer online account opening.
2
Submit KYC Documents
Provide PAN, Aadhaar, passport photo, and fill Form A. The account opens upon initial deposit.
3
Make the Initial Deposit
Minimum ₹500 per year; maximum ₹1,50,000. Deposit any time before 31st March each year.
4
Collect Passbook
Save your PPF passbook and annual deposit receipts to use as investment proof for your employer.

Overview

Section 80D allows deduction on health insurance premiums paid for self, spouse, children, and parents. You can claim up to ₹25,000 for your own family and an additional ₹50,000 if your parents are senior citizens (60+). Preventive health check-up expenses up to ₹5,000 are included within this limit. This deduction is available under the Old Tax Regime.

Key Features
Up to ₹75,000 Combined Deduction
₹25,000 for self + family, plus ₹50,000 for senior citizen parents = total ₹75,000.
Preventive Check-ups Included
Health check-up expenses up to ₹5,000 per year are included within the 80D limit.
Cash Payment Not Allowed
Premium must be paid via non-cash modes (net banking, cheque, UPI) for the deduction to be valid.
Multi-year Policy Deduction
For multi-year policies, deduction is split proportionately over the policy period.

Overview
{% if plan_slug == 'nps' %}

NPS is a government-sponsored pension scheme that helps build a retirement corpus. Beyond the ₹1.5L Section 80C limit, you can claim an additional ₹50,000 deduction under Section 80CCD(1B), making the total potential deduction up to ₹2,00,000. Funds are professionally managed by Pension Fund Managers across equity, corporate bonds, and government securities.

{% elif plan_slug == 'home' %}

A home loan offers tax benefits on two fronts: the principal repayment qualifies under Section 80C (up to ₹1,50,000) and the interest paid is deductible under Section 24(b) (up to ₹2,00,000 for self-occupied property). First-time buyers may also benefit from Section 80EE for an additional ₹50,000, subject to conditions.

{% else %}

Section 80E allows a deduction on the full interest paid on loans taken for higher education. There is no upper limit — the entire interest amount in a year is deductible. The benefit applies for up to 8 consecutive years from the year of first repayment, and covers courses in India and abroad for self, spouse, or children.

{% endif %}
